Subspace Inference for Bayesian Deep Learning

1. summary

Bayesian neural network often involves high dimensional problem over the parameter space. In this paper, they designed a Subspace Inference which conduct dimension reduction over parameter space, then form posterior inference and Bayesian model average. This Subspace Inference shows great performance in terms of accuracy and likelihood and thus implies the low-dim sub-spaces contains a rich representations of full space.

  • PCA
    • train SGD model (go among the weight space). run million iterations, find all the places where gives low loss solutions (W=wi​,wi+T​,..) PCA over snap shot weights. (evidence from previous research, collect good solution among SGD trajectory,equals to find good model among the data)
    • use PCA subspace spanned by SGD trajectory:
      • why SGD trajectory:
        1. diverse set of models with meaningful predictions
        2. cheap to construct.
      • why PCA:
        1. PCA of SGD trajectory correspond to the top eigenvectors of the Hessian of the loss, which captures the sharp directions of the loss surface.
    • PCA over A=UDVT, w^=wSWA​,P=DVT,
      • A=[a1​,…,aM​],ai​=wSWA​−wi​ only store last M deviation. Every c steps update wSWA​, and store its deviations.
    • prior p(z)∼N(0,I)
  • curve subspaces
    • based on previous paper.
      • posterior of NN is nesty. (good solution in the mass)
      • go from 1 good solution to another (cliffs) high mass path. and along the math ,all the solution are good.
      • find the paths, then find the spines which contains this path. (find subspaces which contains the high density of low-loss weight )
    • find paths of near-constant low loss (and high posterior density) in the weight space among converged SGD solutions with different initializations.
